Presence is a mental state in which a user in a Virtual Environment reacts to events in it as if they were real. Traditionally measured through questionnaires and behavioural methods, which offer limited and non-real-time insights, this project proposed an experiment to identify bio-signal correlates of Presence and its components (Place Illusion and Plausibility Illusion) based on Slater's Theory of Presence.

A 2x2 factorial within-subjects experiment was designed, controlling two factors, Immersion and Coherence. Four VR scenarios were built in Unity with in-VR questionnaires and teleportation-based locomotion to control for simulator sickness. Following a pilot study, an experiment was conducted with 20 subjects. EEG and ECG signals were pre-processed and mined to extract features that can measure the level of presence.

Final Year Project, Department of Electronic & Telecommunication Engineering, University of Moratuwa. Group work with N. Suman, S.A.D.O Wickramasooriya and K.I Akalanka.

I worked on the statistical analysis, experiment design, paper writing and biosignal analysis.
The work was accepted at a Q1 Journal (Springer Virtual Reality)
